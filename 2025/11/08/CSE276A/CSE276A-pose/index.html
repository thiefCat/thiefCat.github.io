CSE276A - Euler and Quaternion

If we have constructed two coordinates (e.g., robot base link frame and camera frame), how to describe the transformation between the two coordinates?

Transformation

The offset of the child frame’s origin (camera) from the parent frame’s origin (robot).

Rotation

A useful online rotation representation converter: rotation converter.

Euler Angles

Any 3D orientation can be achieved by performing three sequential rotations around a set of defined axes. The order matters for Euler angles. The most common convention in robotics is Yaw (Z-axis), Pitch (Y-axis), and Roll (X-axis). As a result, a 3-dimensional euler vector cannot describe an orientation unless we specify the rotation order.

Rotation Matrix

A 3x3 matrix where the columns are the child frame’s (camera’s) X, Y, and Z axes, as described in the parent frame’s (robot’s) coordinates. $$ R = \begin{bmatrix} | & | & | \\ \vec{X}_C & \vec{Y}_C & \vec{Z}_C \\ | & | & | \end{bmatrix} $$

Quaternion

Instead of 3 separate rotations, a quaternion represents orientation as a single rotation around one specific axis in 3D space. The quaternion representation is 4 dimensional.

Transformation

After obtaining transformation and rotation, this 4x4 transformation matrix H is constructed by plugging in the 3x3 rotation matrix R and the 3x1 translation vector T.

$$ H = \begin{bmatrix} & & & \\ & R & & \vec{T} \\ & (3 \times 3) & & (3 \times 1) \\ & & & \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$

This matrix H can transform any point PC defined in the camera_frame coordinates directly into its corresponding point PB in the base_link frame coordinates with a single matrix multiplication:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} P_{B,x} \\ P_{B,y} \\ P_{B,z} \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = H \cdot \begin{bmatrix} P_{C,x} \\ P_{C,y} \\ P_{C,z} \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} $$
